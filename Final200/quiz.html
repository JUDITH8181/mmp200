Opioid Addiction Self-Assessment Screening Quiz

This brief questionnaire will evaluate whether you may have developed an unhealthy opiate dependency. Opiates are a class of drug that can include illicit heroin as well as morphine and prescription opioid painkillers such as Vicodin, Opana, OxyContin, Roxicodone, Dilaudid, Zohydro, fentanyl, and others. These questions are based on criteria of the “Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders” for opioid use disorder, which includes abuse of these opiate drugs. *Note: Your responses to these questions are always confidential and fully anonymous.

Do you spend a lot of time acquiring, using, or recovering from the effects of using opiates?
Do you often use opiates for a longer time than you originally intended?
Because of your opiate use, have you failed to meet obligations and responsibilities at home, work, or school?
Have you suffered relationship issues or social problems because of your opiate use and kept using opiates anyway?
Have you been forced to reduce or abandon your social or recreational activities due to your continued use of opiates?
Have you taken opiates when it was physically dangerous to do so, such as while operating a motor vehicle or machinery, or in other unsafe situations?
Have you often wanted to cut back on your use of opiates?
